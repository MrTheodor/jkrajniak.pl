Molecular dynamics simulations of polymerization with forward and backward reactions

Jakub Krajniak Zidan Zhang Sudharsan Pandiyan Erik Nies Giovanni Samaey

Posted on 18 lipca 2016 in articles

We look into the polymerization of polyethylene terephthalate (PET) at the coarse-grained level as an example of a polycondensation reaction with water as a byproduct. To fully simulate this reaction, we develop novel parallel algorithms that allow creating and removing the byproduct molecule appearing as a result of the chemical reactions during the molecular dynamics simulation. We tackle the problem of a smooth byproduct molecule creation by introducing the corresponding forces gradually, via a time-dependent switching function that prevents large increases in the potential energy. Moreover, the implemented methods allow incorporating the reverse process, the hydrolysis reaction, in which the byproduct molecule attacks newly created bonds and reverts to the state before the emission. The methods are implemented in a publicly available software package, and are easily accessible using a newly created domain specific language that describes chemical reactions in an input configuration file.
